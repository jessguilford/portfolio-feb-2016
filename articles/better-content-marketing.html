Want to get better at content marketing?
Stop reading articles about content marketing

Less jargon, more substance.

Have you read an article about content marketing in the last 5 years? I can summarize it for you:

"Provide good content"

Aha! But how?

"Provide good content...by creating high quality content"

Well, that's certainly not wrong, but it is tautological. Personally, I'm not into reading articles that all shill the same boring, vague advice. I bet you're not either.

So, how do we learn to actually create better content? By studying the building blocks of writing, design, and problem-solving. Here are 8 books and websites that have helped me tell better stories, provide more useful information, and make it all look fantastic.

Writing

Everybody Writes - This is a very readable, super comprehensive book that covers the details of how to make your writing more interesting and easier to understand.

Alexandra Franzen - Alexandra is brilliant. Her blog is full of articles on communicating in a more lively, genuine way, plus she offers tons of scripts that you can use as inspiration for structuring your own work.

Design

Know Your Onions - An excellent primer on the fundamentals of design, I highly recommend it for getting familiar with the mechanics of layout, typography, and composition.

The Canva Designschool Blog - The Canva blog is a great mix of inspiration and how-to, and includes tons of articles on creating graphics for social media, email and blogs.

Skillcrush - Skillcrush is geared toward web designers, but the blog has a ton of great resources that are perfect for anyone new to designing stuff for the web.

Problem Solving

Get Bullish - Jen Dziura is downright brilliant, and her advice on marketing, entrepreneurship, and tackling problems creatively can be applied to almost any area of writing or marketing.

Captain Awkward - Captain Awkward addresses interpersonal issues with incredible clarity, steadfast compassion, and solid writing. If you can give your audience the same level of insight, you'll go far.

Six Revisions - If you work in content marketing, you'll probably come up against a tech question at some point. Whether you're looking for ideas for structuring a landing page or you just need to know what an SPF record is, Six Revisions is a good resource to have at hand.
